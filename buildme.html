BuildMe is a habit tracker that aims to help users achieve their goals and break bad habits. Its unique features and user-friendly interface make it stand out from other apps.


I. Overview

Have you ever woken up with the motivation or urge to accomplish a new goal? As you embark on this journey you realize you don't know where to start. You begin to realize it's tough to eliminate bad habits and start new ones. Nowadays, lots of people have turned to habit-tracking apps to help them out. Due to factors like technology fatigue many users find themselves going through the motions and have a hard time staying committed to the process of betterment. In this case study, we will explore the effectiveness of BuildMe and the features my team designed to combat the pain points.

II. Context & Challange

Background and Description

The objective was to design and test a professional mobile application for tracking and building habits. The completed application would allow users to stay committed to building and breaking habits. The project was part of a sprint exercise and was to be completed in one month. Despite the timeline, the project would be open for further development and improvements.

Problem statement

When it comes to setting realistic goals, prioritizing our values, and being accountable how can we motivate and entice users to work on themselves? With the increasing prevalence of distractions and device usage, it's important to find a way to strike a balance in our digital habits. As we adapt to the ever-evolving digital landscape, it's crucial that we approach it with mindfulness and intention.

My solution

As my team discovered habit creation is influenced by a combination of intrinsic and extrinsic factors. So our proposed solution is a well-organized application that implements features built around this fact to help users become aware of these factors and also help them stay focused on habits that can help them reach their goals.

Goals

  1. Help users shape the identity they want to create.

  2. Achieve a platform users can feel enjoyment from.

  3. Help users become aware of their current habits.

  4. Foster a sincere environment good for personal growth.

V. The Process


Research Overview

Before we could start designing our app a great deal of preliminary research had to be done. During our own experiences, we came across the book Atomic Habits by James Clear which we all felt was a good basis for our hypothesis on creating habits. This book would help inform the features we would develop. Our project lead suggested that we follow a sprint format inspired by the book Sprint by Jake Knapp, and we all agreed. We took the time to condense and lay out a structure for our sprint.

We spent some time looking at other habit-tracking apps in the market. We analyzed what worked well and what didn't in these apps. This helped us to get a better understanding of what our app needs to have to be successful.

In addition to analyzing other habit-tracking apps in the market, I researched Habitica, the Forest App and Study Bunny to gather more information on how to create motivating factors during habit creation. I believe we can draw inspiration from these apps, as they offer unique approaches to habit tracking.


Feedback Loop

Mood Board Overview

Our team worked collaboratively to develop mood boards that helped us establish the look and feel of our project. I was responsible for creating a mood board that incorporated shades and tones of yellow and green. These colors were chosen based on their psychological properties and their ability to promote an accepting and active atmosphere. By using these colors, we hope to create an engaging and motivating experience for our users.




User Research & Target Audience

As a team, we wanted to ensure that our habit-tracking application met the needs and expectations of our target audience. To achieve this, we compiled a set of interview questions that covered important demographics and reflections. Additionally, I helped recruit survey participants to gather even more valuable insights. By taking these steps, we hope to create a user-friendly and effective application that truly meets the needs of our users.


V. The Process Continued


Design Overview


Crazy 8's

During our sprint, we utilized a group activity called Crazy 8s. It was a technique that allows us to generate feature ideas and patterns. Once the ideas are generated we iterate on our solutions as many times as possible within an hour of drafting features. It served as a brainstorming session during our sprint.




Feature Ideas

  1. A way for users to document their journey. (A journal, lists, voice recorder)

  2. Reflection tool that allows users to review their mental status. (Questionnaire)

  3. Achievement/trophy/points system.

  4. Interactive character (Tree, pet, avatar) that grows with you.

  5. Social features for users to share their status or progress.

  6. Progress tracker UI too see status. (Graphs, charts or interactive sliders)
